I decided to write up a few things I know about analysing genomic1 data.

There are plenty of reasons for tackling this finally. One selfish one is to become better at certain things. Another one is to enable people to analyse their own data. The time might never come where companies have access to our most precious information, but it might does, and we better know how to analyse the data ourselves.

So that is the overarching goal - to show where to find genomic data, how to interpret/decipher the data, how to analyse it, and how to interpret the results. Lots of promises - I better get started :smile:.

  1. Genetic? Genomic? In my field (statistical genetics) these two terms are used interchangeably. Other fields pay more attention. The definition of genetics is the study of genes and their role in inheritance. Genomics refers more to the science of the full genome (DNA) and how it all fits together. [return]
