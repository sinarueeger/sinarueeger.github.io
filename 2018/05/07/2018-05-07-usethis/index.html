Uses this

What I use to make work work.

Published Mon, May 7, 2018 by Sina R&uuml;eger

Since I was a child I have been interested (if not obsessed) in how people manage their lifes. So recently, when I stumbled accross usesthis it felt like the answer to my main question: what things (tools/apps/hardware) do people use in order to maximise the time for the fun part of work/life.

One of the first usethis entries that I read was the one from Yihui Xie, recorded in 2012. And I wondered what has changed since then in his setup (check other data related interviews here).

I am very pro variation and trying-out. It is not that I am planning to ever reach perfection. It is more a question of change. Not only do I and my environment change over time, but so does hardware and software. For example, for me, taking notes on paper was the best thing to do in 2003. Now it is not anymore.

So here is my personal uses this. I will adapt it over time.

https://twitter.com/SteffLocke/status/990251709531344896

Computer

I started my PhD in 2013 with a Lenovo ThinkPad and Ubuntu. When I realised that I could actually get a computer for free, I got a MacBook Air. I am happy with my choice.

In my group, we do most computations on a linux server that has some storage space and slurm as a scheduling system (Wild west! It’s definitely time for a bingo card).

Backups

Backups are important, and yet I am a bit lazy with that.
Code is mostly on github. And if not, it’s backed up on two other servers. For the backups of my computer I use an WD external hard drive disk. I had two and both eventually died (which might be my fault). The hard disk that still works, is one that I bought back 2011 (!). I really should get one of these again.

Other hardware

I can only work in complete silence (though that depends on stress). To enforce that I use secondhand Bose headphones with rechargable batteries. Because I use them every day (and because it is mostly plastic), the headphones are falling appart. I also use tape or glue for everything that falls apart.

Software

Emacs. I just got used to it.

For R and related file formats I recently switched to RStudio but I still keep emacs on the side (it has been confusing in the beginning, see fully embracing RStudio, search for Emacs-ESS zealot)).

As a browser I use Firefox because I trust them the most. Chrome I only use for three things: meowmeowfacts, to check the R-Ladies Lausanne twitter and the github account (so I won’t have to change the login all the time).

Other apps are: Pocket to organise links to read up on, Trello to organise my lists, Slack for group communication, Keybase for secure communication, Github for projects, Gists for shorter things. All these apps are installed on my phone too.

To blog stuff not related to work, I use medium.

For simple illustrations I use keynote, for manipulating graphs I use AdobeIllustrator (I have tested several other options too, and this seems perfect for now) add tweet https://twitter.com/sinarueeger/status/964851959634198528).

workflow

f.lux and tomighty.

apropos() has never disappointed.

Staying informed

I follow several journals on twitter or get updates by email. I also have some google subscriptions tailored to projects.

I read:

  • The science section of The Atlantic
  • invivo, an excellent science/health from the hospital I work.
  • MIT technology review
  • Mosaic science
  • Quanta magazine
  • lots of blogs

I go through Twitter about once or twice a day, store interesting stuff in Pocket, then sort it out on the weekend.

I listen to:

  • The Broad Experience, a podcast about work + being a woman
  • Not So Standard Deviations (DS)
  • DataFramed (DS)
  • Science: disrupt.
  • Mosaic science podcast
  • I use Castbox on my phone to listen to podcasts.

Mindfulness

Best investment was proabably a meditation cushion. Its a visual reminder of doing meditation. To get into the habit of meditation, I used a Headspace subscription for a while. Right now, I am enjoying Tara Brach talking to me.

Dream setup

  • My computer is always incredibly dirty. I try to clean it, but it won’t get clean. So it would be nice to have some self-clean mode.
